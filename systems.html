What Defines an Artificial Horticulture System?

An artificial horticulture system is not a standalone decorative element.

It is a structured integration of spatial planning, material engineering, compliance alignment, and long-term operational considerations.

A system approach ensures:

  • Structural coordination with architecture
  • Consistent material standards
  • Fire and safety alignment where required
  • Repeatable deployment across multiple sites
  • Reduced long-term maintenance cycles

System Architecture Layers

Each project is structured across defined layers:

1

Spatial Layer

Alignment with ceiling height, load conditions, circulation flow, and visual hierarchy.

2

Structural Layer

Internal reinforcement, trunk engineering, modular canopy configuration.

3

Material Layer

PE composition, UV resistance, surface finishing, fire-ready material options.

4

Compliance Layer

Commercial environment alignment and installation safety standards.

5

Lifecycle Layer

Durability planning and long-term operational stability.

Engineering Note

Each layer interacts with adjacent layers. Material selection affects structural requirements; spatial constraints inform compliance strategy.
